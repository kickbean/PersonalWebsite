About Me

Before joining VISLab, I received my Bachelor's degree from Sichuan University, China in 2009. My research interests lie primarily in the intersection of Data Mining, Machine Learning and Computer Vision. Specifically, I focus on human facial expression analysis and recognition.

Dense Flow-based Face Registration in Video

Align face with non-rigid muscle motion in streaming data.
This technique retains non-rigid muscle motion while aligning facial features. It not only guarantees temporal smoothness, but is also very robust to noise and low image resolution.
This technique is suitable for automatic micro-expression analysis.
Poster Demo Related paper: [C6], Please cite this paper if you use this code.



Reference-Pased Person Re-Identification

Person re-identification is to match individuals captured with different cameras at different time and locations. Instead of matching the individuals from different views directly, we use a reference-set as a bridge for indirect matching. The proposed method is robust against changes in illumination, pose, occlusions in different camera views.
Related paper: [C8]



Unified Face Image Representation for Face Recognition in Videos

Almost all of the camera assignment and handoff techniques largely depend on the results returned by the trackers. In this work, we evaluate the performances of several trackers for doing the camera selection problem. The experiments use real-life data which are collected both indoors and outdoors. We also test the performance at different time and under different weather conditions with different numbers of cameras and persons.
Poster
Related paper: [B1][C4]



Discrete Facial Expressions Recognition
Using Emotion Avatar Image (EAI)

EAI is a image representation of a video. It captures the facial expression information. This representation provides a method to aggregate dynamic information from expressions with various length. It is also robust to out-of-plane head rotation.
"NOTICE": Due to legal issue, the code will not be uploaded. However, if you would like to adopt our technique for coauthorship, send me an email for discussion. We can generate the EAI representations for research purposes.

Error Model for Scene Reconstruction
from Motion and Stereo

Examined the classical perspective camera model, and analytically model the uncertainty in the reconstruction of the 3D position of a scene.
Expressed the uncertainty as a function of the points location in the scene, as well as of the viewpoint change between the two images.
Considered the uncertainty solely from measurement noise, and compute the Cramer-Rao lower bound (CRLB), which provides the highest achievable accuracy any estimator may attain.
